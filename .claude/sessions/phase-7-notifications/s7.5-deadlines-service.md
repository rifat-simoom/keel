## Your task
Implement the deadlines aggregation service and Celery beat scheduler: (1) `backend/services/notifications/deadlines.py` ‚Äî `DeadlineAggregator` class with `async get_deadlines(company_id) -> list[Deadline]`. Aggregates: all VAT quarter deadlines for the next 12 months (from stagger group), CT payment deadline (year_end + 9m1d), CT600 filing deadline (year_end + 12m), self-assessment 31 Jan, invoice due dates (from `invoices` table, status not paid), payroll RTI dates (monthly). Returns sorted list with `days_remaining` and `urgency` field. (2) Celery beat task `check_deadlines()` ‚Äî runs daily at 08:00 UTC, finds deadlines at exactly 30, 7, and 1 days away for all companies, publishes `tax.deadline_approaching` event via outbox for each. (3) Ensure `GET /api/v1/deadlines` and `GET /api/v1/deadlines/next` (already in tax router s6.4) use this aggregator.

## Constraints
- Deadline dates computed from company settings ‚Äî `vat_stagger`, `year_end_month` from DB (FR-7)
- `check_deadlines()` must be idempotent ‚Äî running twice on the same day fires each event once (use Redis dedup key per company+deadline+date)
- All date arithmetic uses `python-dateutil.relativedelta` ‚Äî not manual day counting
- `days_remaining` is always an integer (floor) ‚Äî never float (FP-2 applies to all numeric fields)
- Events published via outbox ‚Äî not direct RabbitMQ calls (FP-8)
- Tax calendar constants (31 Jan self-assessment etc.) from `shared.constants.tax` (FP-9)
- Celery beat schedule: `crontab(hour=8, minute=0)`

## Done when
- `get_deadlines(company_id)` returns deadlines for a Group A company with correct dates
- Deadline at exactly 30 days has `urgency='warning'`, at 7 days `'urgent'`, at 1 day `'critical'`
- `check_deadlines()` publishes `tax.deadline_approaching` for deadlines at 30/7/1 days
- Running `check_deadlines()` twice in one day fires events once (idempotency)
- `pytest backend/services/notifications/tests/test_deadlines.py -v` passes
- `make verify` runs pytest and exits 0
