## Your task
Implement `backend/services/tax/ct_estimator.py` — the `CorpTaxEstimator` class with method `async estimate(company_id, tax_year_end) -> CorpTaxEstimate`. The method must: (1) Sum gross income from all `PAID` invoices in the accounting year (`paid_at` between year-start and `tax_year_end`). (2) Sum allowable expenses: all matched documents with `document_date` in the year and `category` in `CT_ALLOWABLE_CATEGORIES` (which excludes `ENTERTAINMENT` and `OTHER` — FR-2). (3) Apply Annual Investment Allowance for `EQUIPMENT` category expenses up to £1,000,000. (4) Apply the correct CT rate using the marginal relief formula (FR-15) to `taxable_profit`. (5) Return a `CorpTaxEstimate` dataclass: `gross_income`, `total_allowable_expenses`, `capital_aia`, `taxable_profit`, `ct_rate_applied`, `marginal_relief_applied`, `ct_liability`, `tax_year_end`, `payment_deadline` (= year_end + 9 months + 1 day), `ct600_deadline` (= year_end + 12 months).

## Constraints
- All arithmetic: `Decimal` only — never `float` (FP-2)
- CT rates from `shared.constants.tax` — never hardcoded (FP-9)
- Marginal relief formula must be exact: `(250000 - profit) × (profit / 250000) × (0.25 - 0.19)` (FR-15)
- `ENTERTAINMENT` must never appear in allowable expenses — use `CT_ALLOWABLE_CATEGORIES` frozenset (FP-13)
- `EQUIPMENT` over £1,000 uses AIA (up to £1,000,000 limit) — separately tracked from running expenses
- `payment_deadline` calculation: `year_end + relativedelta(months=9) + timedelta(days=1)` — not a rounded approximation
- Estimator recalculates from scratch on every call — no caching at this layer (cache in Redis at API layer)

## Done when
- Profit ≤ £50,000 → 19% rate applied with no marginal relief
- Profit £100,000 → marginal relief formula applied and `marginal_relief_applied = True`
- Profit > £250,000 → 25% rate, no marginal relief
- `ENTERTAINMENT` expenses excluded from calculation
- `payment_deadline` is exactly 9 months and 1 day after year end
- `pytest backend/services/tax/tests/test_ct_estimator.py -v` covers all three rate bands and boundary values (£50,000, £250,000)
- `make verify` runs pytest and exits 0
